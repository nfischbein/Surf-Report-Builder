How it works

Three steps. About ten minutes.

The Builder runs once, in any AI you have access to. After that, you're done with the Builder — you'll run the system it gave you for every session afterward.

  1. 1

    Copy and paste the Builder into your AI.

    Grab the Builder from the section below and paste it into Claude, ChatGPT, or whichever AI you use. The Builder is a long, structured prompt — your AI reads it and knows what to do.

  2. 2

    Answer a few questions about your break.

    Your AI will interview you: which break, when you usually surf, whether you want the quick setup or the full system. Then it researches your local buoys, tide stations, weather sources, and break-specific quirks.

  3. 3

    Get your custom system.

    The Builder generates a set of reports tuned to your spot — Session Reports, Long Range Reports, and an optional tracking system. Save them. Run them whenever you want a read on conditions.

What you'll get

Three reports, your break.

When the Builder finishes setup, it hands you a system with three parts. You run them as often as you want, forever.

01

Session Report

For your next session.

A detailed read for an upcoming surf window — typically tomorrow morning, or whatever session time you set up. Pulls live buoy and tide data, checks the wind forecast, applies your break's local model, and tells you what to expect: size, shape, who the session favors, and what could make the call wrong.

02

Long Range Report

For upcoming swell events.

A 3-to-21-day outlook focused on swell events that might reach your break. Identifies what's developing in upstream basins, when it might arrive, how confident the call is, and which events are worth planning around. Gets less specific the further out it goes — by design.

03

Tracking (optional)

Score your reports. Build your own track record.

After you surf, log a quick score: how close reality felt to what the report predicted. Over time those scores build a personal track record. Eventually the system uses that history to flag patterns and adjust confidence. Skip this if you just want the reports — it's optional.

Data sources

Where the data comes from.

Most AI surf prompts can't check live data. They rely on whatever the model already knows — usually months out of date — or on web searches that pull stale cached pages.

The Builder takes a different approach. When your reports run, they pull from a small server we maintain that fetches data live from official sources, server-side, before your AI sees it. No commercial forecasts. No interpretation. Just the actual numbers.

  • NOAA buoys Live wave height, period, direction, and water temperature.
  • NOAA tide stations Real-time water level and tide predictions.
  • FAA airport weather (METAR) Current wind observations from the airports nearest your break.
  • National Weather Service forecasts Upcoming wind for your session window.

Every reading is timestamped. Every source is named in the report. If a sensor is offline, the report says so instead of guessing.

FAQ

Questions worth asking.

Which AI should I use?

Claude.ai with web search enabled is the recommended runtime. It's what we test against and what handles live data lookups most reliably.

ChatGPT with browsing should also work but we haven't formally tested it yet. Avoid free-tier ChatGPT (no web access) and Perplexity (returns aggressively cached data that can be months out of date).

What does this cost?

Nothing from us. Everything here is free. You'll need an AI subscription if your AI of choice charges for web access — most do — but that's between you and the AI provider.

Do I need to know how to code?

No. If you can copy text, paste it into your AI, and answer a few questions about your break, you can use the Builder.

Why doesn't the Builder just tell me whether to surf today?

Because nobody but you knows what's worth surfing for you. Crowd tolerance, board choice, skill level, what you're trying to work on, whether you have an hour or three — all of it matters, and none of it is something a forecast can know.

The reports describe what's likely to happen at your break in plain English: size, shape, tide, wind, who the session favors, and what could make the call wrong. You make the go/no-go call.

Why don't you use Surfline data?

Surfline and similar commercial sites mix raw data with editorial interpretation — ratings, predicted faces, "go" calls, stylized summaries. The Builder is built to ignore all of that and rely only on the underlying official sources (NOAA, NWS, FAA), which are what Surfline itself ultimately pulls from.

The goal is reports built on raw data and your local knowledge, not on someone else's marketing call.

What if there's no buoy near my break?

Most coastal breaks have a usable buoy somewhere nearby — often farther offshore than you'd expect. The Builder's research step looks for the closest usable stations and builds a local model that accounts for the distance and direction.

Breaks in genuinely instrument-poor areas (some remote stretches of coast) will produce reports with lower confidence, which the system labels honestly rather than hides.

Is my data going anywhere?

The Builder itself doesn't send anything anywhere. Your conversation with your AI stays between you and your AI provider — the same as any other AI conversation.

The optional Tracking system stores your scores in a Google Sheet that you create and own. Nothing is sent to us. Nothing is shared with other users.

What does the optional Tracking actually do?

After you surf, you log a quick score: how close reality felt to what the report predicted, on a scale of -2 to +2. Over time those scores build a personal track record.

Eventually the system can use that history to flag patterns ("similar long-period south swells at this tide have averaged -0.7 in your archive") and adjust confidence accordingly. It's a longer-term feature; the value compounds with every session you log.

Where can I learn more about how it works?

The Manual is a PDF that goes deeper into the design: how the Builder researches your break, why the system avoids commercial forecasts, how the optional tracking archive works, and the philosophy behind the whole approach.
